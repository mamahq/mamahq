Box Breathing
A simple technique used by Navy SEALs and therapists alike to calm the nervous system in minutes.
How it works
Breathe in for 4 counts, hold for 4, breathe out for 4, hold for 4. Repeat 4 rounds. The equal-length phases activate your parasympathetic nervous system — your body's built-in calm response.

Micro-doses of joy
One-minute reset
Tap each movement to learn more.
1
Shoulder rolls
10s
›
Slowly lift both shoulders up toward your ears, roll them back, and let them drop down. Do 3–5 slow rolls.
Feel each shoulder blade squeeze together at the back before releasing.
2
Chin to chest
10s
›
Gently drop your chin toward your chest. Close your eyes and breathe deeply. Feel the stretch along the back of your neck.
Don't force it — just let gravity do the work. Hold for a full breath in and out.
3
Shake out your hands
10s
›
Dangle your arms and shake your wrists loosely for 10 seconds. Let everything go limp — fingers, palms, wrists.
Imagine stress dripping off your fingertips with each shake.
Stand up if you can. Raise both arms overhead, fingers spread wide. Stretch up as tall as you can and hold for a breath.
You've been here. You're still standing. That counts for something.
